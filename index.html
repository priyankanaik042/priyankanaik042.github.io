MYERS-BRIGGS :: "INTJ"

INTJs are analytical problem-solvers, eager to improve systems and processes with their innovative ideas. They have a knack for seeing possibilities for improvement, whether at work, at home, or in themselves.

Often intellectual, INTJs enjoy logical reasoning and complex problem-solving. They approach life by analyzing the theory behind what they see, and are typically focused inward, on their own thoughtful study of the world around them.

INTJs are drawn to logical systems. They are typically independent and selective about their relationships, preferring to associate with people who they find intellectually stimulating.

INTJs form just two percent of the population, and women of this personality type are especially rare.
